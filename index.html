About

What is Bone Biopsy

Bone biopsy involves a procedure to insert a biopsy needle (known as Jamshidi Needle) and cannula inside the bone. This insertion of the needle is manual. This insertion is not at all controlled and fully manual hence a lot of care needs to be taken while doing bone biopsy to avoid the risk of bone fracture, tumor contamination. In present invention we tried to eliminated the risk of bone fracture by precisely controlling pressure for inserting the needle by a surgeon or operator during bone biopsy procedure.

The innovation lies in the process where a manual bone biopsy process has been converted to a fully- automatic process by using controlled machine derived mechanism. It uses a powered device and hence surgeon need not to put force, further we are working to get this into tele robotic procedure. The present device in current form has been granted Indian patent and thus have a novelty and non-obvious solution which is a pre-requisite for any patent.

The present bone biopsy device is a result of the specific issues faced by performing thousands of biopsies carried out by one of our founder Dr. Abhijeet Salunke.

I Bone Biopsy

i Bone Biopsy Device

We a team of two orthopedic onco surgeons and two engineers identified the limitation of the current Bone Biopsy process which is a manual process that results in inaccurate results in 1st attempt, and designed an intelligent biopsy device that can perform the procedure with good yield in the 1st time with less discomfort to the patient and ease of handing to Surgeon.

The device would benefit ortho-surgeons, onco-ortho-surgeons, and medical practitioner who perform bone biopsy of any part of the body for diagnosis. It will help to ease the process by making it automatic. The ultimate benefit will be to patients where the residue pain will be reduced.

Current System Limitations

  • • Current process of performing bone biopsies, particularly for bone tumors, bone marrow biopsy, and related orthopaedic procedures, presents several challenges such as:
  • • It requires significant manual effort.
  • • Risk of complications such as bone fractures, contamination of tumor cells, bleeding, infection, and damage to nerves and blood vessels.
  • • It is a painful procedure.
  • • It is complex and demanding when tumors are located within the medullary cavity with intact cortical bone.
  • • All these factors compromise patient safety and well-being.

Proposed Solution

  • • The product is a drill machine type tool having many features such as:
  • • Controlled forward motion and controlled rotation for drilling operation.
  • • Feedback of force and current for optimized drilling control.
  • • Automatic stopping mechanism upon detecting cortical breach.
  • • Accurate and high yield of bone marrow specimen in a single attempt.

Product

Bone Biopsy Device

This proposed intelligent bone drill and biopsy (I-bone biopsy) device will obtain good amount of tissue for diagnosis of disease like bone cancer, bone marrow diseases including infection, blood cancers (leukaemia), leukopenia, leukocytosis, thrombocytopenia, thrombocytosis, pancytopenia and polycythemia with higher accuracy and safety. The expert also suggests bone marrow examination for cases such as (a) Fevers of unknown origin (b) Anemia (c) Hemochromatosis. The histopathological diagnosis is required for proper treatment. Expert may suggest with chemotherapy, surgery and radiation treatment based on the report. This device will make bone biopsy procedure minimally morbidity ( pain, residual pain and discomfort) to patient with excellent tissue for diagnosis of the diseases by the pathologist.

This i-bone drill biopsy device is a fully controlled, motorized, and powered system for bone biopsy with unique depth, current and pressure sensors leading to precise, perfect bone / bone marrow specimen collection that will be used for diagnosis of diseases (bone cancer and infections and bone marrow abnormalities). This device will reduce morbidity to the patient following bone biopsy by obtaining long tissue cores with limited penetrations into the affected bone and risk of fracture.
